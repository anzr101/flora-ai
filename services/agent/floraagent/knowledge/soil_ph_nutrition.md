# Soil, pH, and Nutrition

## Soil pH
- Most houseplants prefer **slightly acidic to neutral soil, pH 6.0–7.0**.
- Outside this range, nutrients become chemically "locked" even when present —
  high pH (alkaline) commonly causes iron deficiency (interveinal yellowing).
- The FloraAI sensors report soil pH. A deviation of more than ~1 unit from 6.5
  is a meaningful stressor and a frequent driver of "at_risk" predictions.
- Lower pH with elemental sulfur or peat; raise it with garden lime. Adjust
  gradually.

## Soil structure
- Drainage matters as much as nutrition. Heavy, compacted soil holds water and
  causes root rot. Add perlite, bark, or coarse sand for aeration.
- Succulents need a gritty, fast-draining mix; tropical foliage prefers a
  moisture-retentive but airy mix.

## Fertiliser
- Plants need nitrogen (leaf growth), phosphorus (roots/flowers), and potassium
  (overall vigour), plus micronutrients.
- **Less is more.** Over-fertilising burns roots and causes brown leaf tips and
  a white salt crust on the soil. Both neglect and over-feeding show up as
  stress in the health model.
- A balanced approach for foliage plants: a diluted balanced liquid feed about
  twice a month during the growing season (spring–summer), and little to none in
  autumn/winter.
- Flush the soil with plain water occasionally to wash out accumulated salts.
