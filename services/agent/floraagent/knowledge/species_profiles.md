# Common Species Profiles

## Monstera deliciosa (Swiss cheese plant)
- Light: bright indirect. Water: top 2–3 cm dry between waterings.
- Likes a moss pole to climb; fenestrations (leaf holes) increase with maturity
  and good light. Toxic to cats and dogs if ingested (calcium oxalate).

## Pothos (Epipremnum aureum)
- Nearly indestructible. Tolerates low light but grows faster in bright indirect.
- Let the top soil dry; very forgiving of occasional neglect. Toxic to pets.

## Snake plant (Dracaena trifasciata)
- Extremely drought tolerant; water sparingly, let dry fully. Tolerates low
  light. Overwatering is the only common way to kill it. Mildly toxic to pets.

## Peace lily (Spathiphyllum)
- Bright indirect light. Droops dramatically when thirsty and recovers quickly
  after watering. Brown tips usually mean over-fertilising or tap-water
  fluoride/chlorine. Toxic to cats and dogs.

## Fiddle-leaf fig (Ficus lyrata)
- Bright indirect light, hates being moved and hates drafts. Water when the top
  few cm dry. Brown spots can mean either over- or under-watering; check roots.

## Calathea / Maranta (prayer plants)
- High humidity, consistently moist soil, no direct sun. Sensitive to hard tap
  water (use filtered/rain water). Crispy edges signal dry air or mineral
  buildup. Non-toxic to pets.

## Aloe vera
- Bright light, gritty fast-draining soil, infrequent deep watering. A succulent;
  treat like a cactus. The gel is a traditional topical remedy for minor burns.

## Basil (Ocimum basilicum)
- Edible herb. Lots of light (6+ hours), consistently moist soil, pinch flower
  buds to prolong leaf production. Non-toxic, culinary.
