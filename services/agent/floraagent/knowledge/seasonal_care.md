# Seasonal Care

Plants experience seasons indoors through changing light and temperature.

## Spring
- Growth resumes. Resume regular fertilising, repot root-bound plants, and take
  cuttings. Watering frequency increases as days lengthen.

## Summer
- Peak growth and water demand. Watch for heat stress near hot windows; soil
  dries faster. Increase humidity for tropicals; watch for spider mites in dry
  heat.

## Autumn
- Growth slows. Taper off fertiliser. Reduce watering frequency as light drops.
  Move sun-lovers closer to windows to compensate for weaker light.

## Winter
- Most foliage plants are semi-dormant. **Water much less** — wet soil in cold,
  low light is the leading cause of winter root rot. Stop fertilising. Keep
  plants away from cold drafts and radiators. Expect little to no new growth;
  this is normal, not a problem to "fix" with more water or feed.

## A seasonal rule of thumb
Match inputs (water, feed) to the plant's activity. When growth slows in low
light, inputs must slow too — otherwise unused water and nutrients accumulate and
cause damage.
