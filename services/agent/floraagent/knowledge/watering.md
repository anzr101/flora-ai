# Watering Fundamentals

Most houseplants are killed by overwatering, not underwatering. Roots need
oxygen; constantly saturated soil suffocates them and invites root rot.

## How to judge when to water
- **Finger test:** insert a finger 2–3 cm into the soil. Water only if it is dry
  at that depth for most foliage plants.
- **Weight test:** lift the pot. A dry pot is noticeably lighter; with practice
  this is the most reliable cue.
- **Soil moisture:** for the FloraAI sensors, 20–35% soil moisture usually means
  it is time to water most tropical foliage; below 15% is drought stress; above
  60% sustained risks rot.

## Technique
- Water thoroughly until it drains from the bottom, then empty the saucer.
  Shallow watering leaves the lower root ball dry and pushes roots upward.
- Use room-temperature water. Cold water shocks tropical roots.
- Reduce watering in winter when light and growth slow.

## Plant-type guidance
- **Succulents & cacti:** soak then let dry completely. Water roughly every
  2–3 weeks indoors; far less in winter.
- **Tropical foliage (pothos, monstera, philodendron):** keep lightly moist, let
  the top 2–3 cm dry between waterings.
- **Ferns & calatheas:** prefer consistently moist (not soggy) soil and high
  humidity; they brown at the edges when too dry.

## Symptoms
- **Overwatering:** yellowing lower leaves, mushy stems, fungus gnats, a sour
  soil smell, wilting *despite* wet soil.
- **Underwatering:** crispy brown leaf edges, drooping that recovers fast after
  watering, soil pulling away from the pot edge.
