# Light and Temperature

Light is the single biggest driver of indoor plant health after watering.

## Light levels
- **Bright direct:** sunlight hits the leaves for several hours (south-facing
  windows). Good for cacti, succulents, citrus; scorches shade plants.
- **Bright indirect:** the room is bright but the sun does not strike leaves
  directly. Ideal for most tropical foliage (monstera, philodendron, pothos).
- **Low light:** away from windows. Tolerated (not loved) by snake plants, ZZ
  plants, pothos. Growth slows and variegation fades.

## Reading light problems
- **Too little light:** leggy growth reaching toward the window, small new
  leaves, loss of variegation, slow growth, soil staying wet too long.
- **Too much direct light:** bleached or scorched pale-brown patches, crispy
  leaf tips, faded colour.

The FloraAI sensors report daily light hours. For most foliage plants, 8–12
hours of bright indirect light per day is a healthy target. Under 4 hours
typically causes etiolation (stretching).

## Temperature
- Most houseplants are comfortable at **18–27 °C**.
- Avoid cold drafts, single-pane windows in winter, and hot air vents.
- Sudden swings stress plants more than a steady non-ideal temperature.
- Heat above ~30 °C combined with dry soil is especially damaging — heat stress
  is much worse when the plant cannot transpire and cool itself.
