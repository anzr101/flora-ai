# Common Diseases and Pests

## Fungal & bacterial diseases
- **Root rot (Pythium/Phytophthora):** caused by overwatering and poor drainage.
  Symptoms: wilting despite wet soil, black/mushy roots, yellow lower leaves.
  Action: remove from pot, cut away rotten roots, repot in fresh well-draining
  mix, reduce watering.
- **Powdery mildew:** white powdery coating on leaves in humid, stagnant air.
  Action: improve airflow, remove affected leaves, apply a fungicide or potassium
  bicarbonate spray.
- **Leaf spot (fungal/bacterial):** brown/black spots often with yellow halos.
  Action: remove affected foliage, avoid wetting leaves, increase airflow.
- **Anthracnose:** dark sunken lesions on leaves/stems, common in warm wet
  conditions. Action: prune affected tissue, keep foliage dry.

## Pests
- **Spider mites:** fine webbing, stippled yellow speckling; thrive in hot dry
  air. Action: raise humidity, rinse leaves, use insecticidal soap or neem.
- **Mealybugs:** white cottony clusters in leaf joints. Action: dab with
  isopropyl alcohol, treat with neem.
- **Fungus gnats:** small flies from consistently wet soil. Action: let soil dry
  more between waterings, use sticky traps, a layer of sand on top.
- **Scale:** brown bumps on stems/leaves. Action: scrape off, treat with neem or
  horticultural oil.

## Prevention
Most disease is an environmental problem first. Good airflow, appropriate
watering, clean tools, and quarantining new plants prevent the majority of
outbreaks. A plant flagged "at_risk" or "diseased" by the ML model should first
be checked for overwatering and poor drainage.
